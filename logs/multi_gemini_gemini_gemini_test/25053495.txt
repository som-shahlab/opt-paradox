Treatment:  Appendectomy is the definitive treatment for acute appendicitis. This can be performed laparoscopically or open, depending on the severity of the inflammation and surgeon preference.  Preoperatively, the patient should receive intravenous fluids and antibiotics with coverage for gram-negative and anaerobic bacteria (e.g., cefoxitin, ertapenem, or a combination of a third-generation cephalosporin and metronidazole).  Postoperatively, the patient should receive pain management, continue antibiotics as indicated, and be monitored for complications such as infection or abscess formation.
