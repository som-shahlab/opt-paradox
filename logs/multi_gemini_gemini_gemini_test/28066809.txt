Treatment:  The patient requires immediate treatment for DKA. This includes:
1. Fluid resuscitation with isotonic saline (0.9% NaCl) to restore intravascular volume.
2. Intravenous insulin infusion to correct hyperglycemia and ketosis.  Start with a continuous infusion of 0.1 units/kg/hour.
3. Electrolyte monitoring and replacement, particularly potassium, as insulin therapy can shift potassium intracellularly, leading to hypokalemia.
4. Monitoring of vital signs, mental status, and urine output.
5. Once the patient is stabilized and the glucose level is below 200 mg/dL, switch to subcutaneous insulin therapy and add dextrose to the IV fluids to prevent hypoglycemia.
6. Investigate the underlying cause of DKA, which may be a new onset of type 1 diabetes or an infection in a patient with known diabetes.
7. After the acute episode resolves, patient education on diabetes management, including insulin administration, blood glucose monitoring, and sick day management, is crucial.

Once the patient is stable, further investigations, including amylase, lipase, troponin, and EKG, should be performed to rule out other potential diagnoses.
