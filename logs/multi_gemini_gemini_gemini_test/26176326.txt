4. **Nil per os (NPO):**  Withhold oral intake to allow the pancreas to rest.
5. **Monitoring:** Closely monitor vital signs, fluid balance, and laboratory values (including lipase, amylase, liver function tests, and renal function).
6. **Biliary imaging:**  Abdominal ultrasound or magnetic resonance cholangiopancreatography (MRCP) to confirm the presence of gallstones and assess the biliary tree for obstruction.

8. **Cholecystectomy:**  After the acute pancreatitis resolves, cholecystectomy is recommended to prevent recurrent episodes.  This may be performed laparoscopically once the patient is stable.
9. **Antibiotics:**  While not routinely indicated in uncomplicated gallstone pancreatitis, antibiotics may be considered in cases of severe pancreatitis or suspected cholangitis.
10. **Nutritional support:** If the patient remains NPO for an extended period, enteral nutrition via a nasogastric or nasojejunal feeding tube should be initiated.  Parenteral nutrition may be necessary in cases of severe pancreatitis or intolerance to enteral feeding.
