Treatment: The patient should be admitted for supportive management of acute pancreatitis. This includes:
* **NPO (nothing by mouth):** To allow the pancreas to rest.
* **IV fluids:** To maintain hydration and electrolyte balance.
* **Pain management:**  Intravenous opioids (e.g., morphine, hydromorphone) are typically required for pain control.
* **Monitoring:** Close monitoring of vital signs, fluid balance, and laboratory values (including lipase, amylase, electrolytes, and complete blood count) is essential.

* **Treatment of underlying cause:** If gallstones are confirmed as the cause of pancreatitis, cholecystectomy may be necessary once the acute pancreatitis resolves.
