* **Bowel rest:**  Clear liquid diet initially, advancing to a low-residue diet as tolerated.
* **Antibiotics:**  Coverage for gram-negative and anaerobic bacteria is recommended.  A combination of ciprofloxacin and metronidazole is a common choice, or alternatively, amoxicillin-clavulanate can be used.  Duration of antibiotics is typically 7-10 days.
* **Pain management:**  Acetaminophen or NSAIDs for mild to moderate pain.  Opioids may be necessary for severe pain, but should be used judiciously.
* **Hydration:**  Encourage oral fluids or IV fluids if necessary.
* **Close monitoring:**  Monitor for worsening symptoms such as increasing pain, fever, or signs of peritonitis.  Repeat CT scan may be indicated if symptoms worsen or do not improve within a few days.
