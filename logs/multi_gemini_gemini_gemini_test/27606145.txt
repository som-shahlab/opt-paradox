1. Acute Appendicitis
2. Mesenteric Adenitis
3. Right Lower Lobe Pneumonia
4. Ovarian Cyst (if female)
5. Ureterolithiasis

Treatment:  Appendectomy is the definitive treatment for acute appendicitis.  This can be performed laparoscopically or open, with laparoscopic appendectomy being the preferred approach in most cases due to its minimally invasive nature, reduced postoperative pain, and faster recovery.  The patient should be started on intravenous fluids and broad-spectrum antibiotics to cover common enteric bacteria.  Pain management should be addressed with appropriate analgesics.  Postoperative care includes monitoring for complications such as infection, bleeding, and ileus.  The patient should be advised to avoid strenuous activity and heavy lifting for several weeks following surgery.
