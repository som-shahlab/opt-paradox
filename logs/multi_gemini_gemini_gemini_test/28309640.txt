Treatment: The patient should be treated for gallstone pancreatitis with the following measures:

* **NPO (nothing by mouth):**  Initially, to allow the pancreas to rest.
* **IV fluids:**  To maintain hydration and electrolyte balance.
* **Pain management:**  With intravenous analgesics such as morphine or hydromorphone.
* **Anti-emetics:**  To control nausea and vomiting.
* **Monitoring:**  Closely monitor vital signs, including heart rate, blood pressure, and oxygen saturation.  Also, monitor laboratory values, including amylase, lipase, complete blood count, and electrolytes.
* **Cholecystectomy:**  Surgical removal of the gallbladder is recommended after the acute pancreatitis subsides to prevent recurrence. This can often be performed laparoscopically.
* **ERCP (Endoscopic Retrograde Cholangiopancreatography):** If there is evidence of biliary obstruction or cholangitis, ERCP may be necessary to remove the obstructing stone.
* **Management of GERD:** Once the acute pancreatitis is resolved, the patient's GERD should be addressed with lifestyle modifications (e.g., avoiding trigger foods, weight loss) and medications such as proton pump inhibitors (PPIs).
